Bajra (Pearl Millet)
•	Planting Time: June to July (Kharif season).
•	Soil: Drought-tolerant, grows well in sandy loam soil.
•	Irrigation: Mostly rainfed; no irrigation required unless there's severe drought.
•	Fertilization: Apply nitrogen-based fertilizers at sowing and tillering stages.
•	Harvesting: October to November.
•	Yield Improvement Tips:
o	Use drought-resistant varieties.
o	Practice crop rotation to improve soil health.
•	Bajra (Pearl Millet) Farming Steps:
o	Step 1: Seed Selection: Use drought-resistant varieties.
o	Step 2: Land Preparation: Deep plowing followed by harrowing.
o	Step 3: Sowing: Sow seeds 2.5 cm deep with 45 cm spacing between rows.
o	Step 4: Irrigation: Mostly rainfed, but irrigate during severe drought.
o	Step 5: Fertilization: Apply nitrogen fertilizers during sowing and tillering stages.
o	Step 6: Pest Management: Control pests like stem borers using chemical or organic methods.
o	Step 7: Harvesting: Harvest when heads turn brown, and grains are hard.
