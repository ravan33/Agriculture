Chilies
•	Planting Time: June to July (Kharif season).
•	Soil: Well-drained sandy loam to clay loam soil.
•	Irrigation: Regular irrigation is needed; avoid waterlogging.
•	Fertilization: Apply basal doses of nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium fertilizers.
•	Pest Control: Regular monitoring for pests like aphids, thrips, and whiteflies.
•	Harvesting: November to December.
•	Yield Improvement Tips:
o	Use pest-resistant and high-yielding varieties.
o	Maintain proper spacing and regular weeding.
•	Chilies Farming Steps:
o	Step 1: Seed Selection: Use high-yielding, pest-resistant varieties.
o	Step 2: Nursery Bed Preparation: Prepare nursery beds and sow seeds. Water regularly until transplanting.
o	Step 3: Transplanting: Transplant seedlings after 30-40 days in the main field with 60 cm row spacing.
o	Step 4: Irrigation: Regular irrigation, especially during flowering and fruiting stages.
o	Step 5: Fertilization: Use NPK fertilizers based on soil testing results.
o	Step 6: Pest Management: Monitor and control pests like thrips, aphids, and fruit borers.
o	Step 7: Harvesting: Harvest fruits when fully matured and red.
