Groundnut
•	Planting Time:
o	Kharif: June to July
o	Rabi: October to November
•	Soil: Well-drained sandy loam.
•	Irrigation: Needs 2-3 irrigations during critical stages (flowering and pod formation).
•	Fertilization: Apply organic manure (FYM) and NPK; apply gypsum for better pod filling.
•	Harvesting:
o	Kharif: October to November
o	Rabi: February to March
•	Yield Improvement Tips:
o	Regular weeding.
o	Monitor for pests like leaf miners and aphids.
•	Groundnut Farming Steps:
o	Step 1: Seed Selection: Use high-yielding, disease-resistant varieties.
o	Step 2: Land Preparation: Deep plowing and leveling. Ensure loose soil for easy penetration of pods.
o	Step 3: Sowing: Sow seeds 5 cm deep with a spacing of 30 cm between rows and 10 cm between plants.
o	Step 4: Irrigation: Provide 2-3 irrigations during flowering and pod formation stages.
o	Step 5: Fertilization: Apply gypsum during pegging for better pod formation. Use NPK fertilizers.
o	Step 6: Weed Control: Use manual weeding or herbicides at regular intervals.
o	Step 7: Pest Management: Control leaf miners and aphids.
o	Step 8: Harvesting: Harvest when 70% of the pods have matured, usually after 120-130 days.
