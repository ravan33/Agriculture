Red Gram (Pigeon Pea)
•	Planting Time: June to July (Kharif season).
•	Soil: Loamy soil with good drainage.
•	Irrigation: Mostly rainfed; irrigation only in extreme drought conditions.
•	Fertilization: Phosphorus and potassium-rich fertilizer; Rhizobium inoculation to promote nitrogen fixation.
•	Harvesting: January to February.
•	Yield Improvement Tips:
o	Timely sowing with seed treatment.
o	Use pest-resistant varieties.
o	Monitor for pod borer pests.
•	Red Gram (Pigeon Pea) Farming Steps:
o	Step 1: Seed Selection: Use high-yielding or disease-resistant varieties.
o	Step 2: Land Preparation: Deep plowing followed by leveling.
o	Step 3: Sowing: Sow seeds 5 cm deep with a spacing of 75 cm between rows and 30 cm between plants.
o	Step 4: Irrigation: Typically rainfed, but supplemental irrigation may be needed during droughts.
o	Step 5: Fertilization: Apply phosphorus-based fertilizers; consider Rhizobium seed inoculation.
o	Step 6: Weed Control: Hand weeding at intervals or use herbicides.
o	Step 7: Pest Management: Control pod borers and other pests using IPM methods.
o	Step 8: Harvesting: Harvest when pods have turned brown and seeds are hard.
