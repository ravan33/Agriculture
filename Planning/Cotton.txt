Cotton
•	Planting Time: June to July (Kharif season).
•	Soil: Black cotton soil (well-drained) is ideal.
•	Irrigation: Grown rainfed or with supplemental irrigation; water stress must be avoided during flowering.
•	Fertilization: Apply basal doses of NPK; supplement with micronutrients if needed.
•	Pest Control: Use BT cotton varieties for better pest resistance; control pests like bollworm.
•	Harvesting: November to February.
•	Yield Improvement Tips:
o	Spacing and proper pruning.
o	Integrated pest and weed management.
•	Cotton Farming Steps:
o	Step 1: Seed Selection: Choose BT cotton or hybrid varieties.
o	Step 2: Land Preparation: Deep plowing and leveling the field. Ensure good drainage.
o	Step 3: Sowing: Sow seeds 1 inch deep with a spacing of 75 cm between rows and 45 cm between plants.
o	Step 4: Irrigation: Provide water as per requirement, avoiding water stress during flowering and boll formation.
o	Step 5: Fertilization: Apply basal doses of nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium fertilizers. Add micro-nutrients like zinc if needed.
o	Step 6: Weed Control: Use pre-emergence herbicides and manual weeding as required.
o	Step 7: Pest Management: Monitor for pests like pink bollworm. Use IPM strategies.
o	Step 8: Growth Regulation: Use growth regulators to manage plant height and increase boll setting.
o	Step 9: Harvesting: Pick the cotton when 80-90% of the bolls have opened.
