Maize (Corn)
•	Planting Time:
o	Kharif: June to July
o	Rabi: October to November (in irrigated areas)
•	Soil: Well-drained loamy soil.
•	Irrigation: Requires 3-4 irrigations during critical growth stages.
•	Fertilization: Balanced NPK application based on soil test results; nitrogen is especially critical.
•	Harvesting:
o	Kharif: October to November
o	Rabi: February to March
•	Yield Improvement Tips:
o	Use hybrid maize varieties.
o	Regular weeding and pest control (e.g., stem borers).
•	Maize (Corn) Farming Steps:
o	Step 1: Seed Selection: Select high-yielding hybrid varieties.
o	Step 2: Land Preparation: Deep plowing followed by leveling.
o	Step 3: Sowing: Sow seeds 2.5-5 cm deep with spacing of 60 cm between rows and 20 cm between plants.
o	Step 4: Irrigation: Provide irrigation immediately after sowing, during tasseling, and grain filling stages.
o	Step 5: Fertilization: Apply nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium based on soil testing (e.g., 50% nitrogen at sowing, the rest during the vegetative stage).
o	Step 6: Weed Control: Use pre-emergence herbicides and manual weeding.
o	Step 7: Pest Management: Control pests like stem borers using chemical or biological methods.
o	Step 8: Harvesting: Harvest when the grains are hard and the cobs have dried.
