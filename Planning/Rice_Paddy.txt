Rice (Paddy)
•	Planting Time:
o	Kharif: June to July (with onset of monsoon)
o	Rabi: November to December (in irrigated areas)
•	Soil: Clayey loam to sandy loam with good water retention.
•	Irrigation: Requires regular flooding or irrigation.
•	Fertilization: Apply nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium fertilizers (based on soil test).
•	Harvesting:
o	Kharif: October to November
o	Rabi: March to April
•	Yield Improvement Tips:
o	Use high-yield varieties.
o	Ensure timely transplanting and water management.
o	Integrated pest management (IPM) for diseases like blast and bacterial leaf blight.
•	Rice (Paddy) Farming Steps:
o	Step 1: Seed Selection: Choose high-yielding or disease-resistant varieties.
o	Step 2: Seedbed Preparation: Prepare a nursery bed and soak seeds for 24 hours before sowing.
o	Step 3: Nursery Sowing: Sow seeds in the nursery bed and water regularly.
o	Step 4: Land Preparation: Plow the field, level it, and flood with water.
o	Step 5: Transplanting: After 25-30 days, transplant the seedlings into the field at a spacing of 20 cm between rows.
o	Step 6: Water Management: Maintain 2-5 cm of standing water until panicle initiation.
o	Step 7: Fertilization: Apply NPK (Nitrogen-Phosphorus-Potassium) fertilizers at intervals (e.g., 50% nitrogen at transplanting, the rest during tillering and panicle initiation).
o	Step 8: Weed Management: Use herbicides or manual weeding.
o	Step 9: Pest and Disease Control: Monitor for pests like stem borers and diseases like bacterial blight.
o	Step 10: Harvesting: Harvest 30-40 days after flowering when grains turn golden.
